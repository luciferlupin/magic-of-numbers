1. Introduction to the Vastu Purusha Mandala

The concept of Vastu Shastra is rooted in the Vastu Purusha Mandala—the metaphysical planning grid representing the cosmic body of the Vastu Purusha, who was pinned down by the gods to bring order to empty space. Every building, room, and apartment block possesses this subtle energetic grid. When architectural coordinate structures align perfectly with the cardinal directions (North, East, South, West) and their intermediate zones, it enables cosmic energy (Prana) to rotate smoothly.

When elements are blocked or positioned in conflicting zones (for example, placing a fire element like a stove in a water zone like the North-East), it creates structural tension. This handbook provides simple, non-destructive Vastu remedies to correct these imbalances.

Historically, Vedic architects (Sthapatis) designed palaces, public temples, and residences using these core mathematical proportions (Ayadi Shadvarga formulas) to sync structural volumes with the owner's birth stars. By balancing the five elements (Pancha Bhootas: Earth, Water, Fire, Air, and Space), Vastu establishes a harmonious resonance between human biological rhythms and nature's planetary grid lines.
